About Me

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at DAV University, Jalandhar, Punjab, India, with over a decade of teaching and research experience. My expertise lies in Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Nature-Inspired Metaheuristic Algorithms, and Medical Image Analysis.

My Ph.D. research focused on developing innovative nature-inspired metaheuristic algorithms for feature selection in machine learning . I conducted extensive experiments on various standard datasets of different sizes to assess the performance of the algorithms. Subsequently, I applied these algorithms to challenging medical imaging problems, specifically breast density and breast mass classification in mammogram images to aid in the early detection and screening of breast abnormalities.

A notable highlight of my work is an international collaboration with Prof. Uwe Aickelin (University of Melbourne), where we developed AI-based optimization models that significantly enhanced the accuracy and reliability of diabetic retinopathy diagnosis. Building on this foundation, my research now focuses on creating adaptive, interpretable, and clinically deployable AI frameworks that integrate metaheuristic optimization, transfer learning, and vision transformer-based architectures. This approach addresses critical challenges in medical image analysis, including dataset bias, model generalizability, and robustness across heterogeneous clinical environments. Looking ahead, my long-term vision is to advance AI systems that are technologically innovative, ethically sound, and capable of supporting early and reliable disease detection, enabling healthcare professionals to make informed decisions for conditions such as diabetic retinopathy, breast cancer, and respiratory illnesses. In teaching, I emphasize critical thinking, problem-solving, and research-driven learning, fostering a student-centric environment that bridges theory with practice and prepares students to contribute to the next generation of AI-driven solutions in medical imaging and healthcare. In teaching, I strive to cultivate critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and research-oriented learning.
